### Long Term

In the long term, I'm looking forward to moving some capital to commercial real estate (apartment buildings) and into dividend paying ETFs or companies that I can write calls.

The ETFs include QYLD, RYLD, and XYLD holding the Nasdaq, Russell and SP respectively. Others are NUSI (covered puts) and JEPI (covered calls + ELNs for income). They each pay an annual between 9-12% on a monthly distribution. Here's a breakdown of their yields:

| 52 week return | -0.31% |  9.45% | 10.19% | 1.64% | 14.10% |
| 52 week yield  | 11.36% | 11.88% |  8.86% | 7.76% |  5.82% |

| expense        |  0.60% |  0.60% |  0.60% | 0.68% |  0.35% |


Note that while these expense ratios here are reasonably high, only at points above $5M (~30k) will managing these covered calls will be valuable unless you either already have a portfolio with a large amount of shares (in which case, you should already be writing calls), or you would like to start taking directional bets.
